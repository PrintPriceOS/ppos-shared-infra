# Target Investors (EU & US) for PrintRisk Infrastructure

This curated list focuses on VCs and strategic funds that understand **Vertical SaaS, B2B Infrastructure, Automation, and Developer Tools**. They are much more likely to underwrite a highly technical Moat (like RIP-grade preflighting) than generalist consumer funds.

---

## 🇪🇺 Europe (Early Stage / Seed / Series A)

### 1. Point Nine Capital (Berlin)
**Why:** The undisputed kings of early-stage SaaS and B2B marketplaces in Europe. If it's a B2B SaaS wedge that becomes infrastructure, they want to see it.
**Focus:** B2B SaaS, Marketplaces.

### 2. Crane Venture Partners (London)
**Why:** Deep enterprise and infrastructure focus. They love founders building complex middleware and backend automation layers.
**Focus:** Enterprise software, Data, Deep tech infrastructure.

### 3. Episode 1 Ventures (London)
**Why:** Focused on B2B software infrastructure and deep automation.
**Focus:** Deep tech, B2B software, Automation.

### 4. LocalGlobe / Latitude (London)
**Why:** One of the most active early-stage funds in the UK with a strong track record of backing highly technical B2B platforms.
**Focus:** Agnostic, strong B2B track record.

### 5. Kima Ventures (Paris)
**Why:** One of the most active angel/seed funds globally. They move extremely fast and have backed hundreds of SaaS infrastructure plays.
**Focus:** Agnostic, high velocity.

### 6. Speedinvest (Vienna / London / Berlin)
**Why:** Dedicated B2B software and Industrial tech teams. They understand the translation of physical workflows (print) into digital automation.
**Focus:** SaaS, Enterprise tech, Industrial software.

### 7. Seedcamp (London)
**Why:** Premier European accelerator/seed fund. They’ve backed UIPath, Hopin, and other massive global automation and infrastructure success stories.
**Focus:** Pre-seed / Seed SaaS and Infra.

### 8. Connect Ventures (London)
**Why:** High focus on product-led growth (PLG) and B2B SaaS. If your UX/UI is beautiful and solves a hard technical problem, they are a great fit.
**Focus:** PLG, B2B SaaS.

### 9. Hoxton Ventures (London)
**Why:** Strong European roots but massive US network. They back European founders building global infrastructure companies from day one.
**Focus:** Enterprise, Deep tech.

### 10. Chalfen Ventures (London)
**Why:** Mike Chalfen is a legendary solo GP who backs highly technical, un-obvious B2B software that dominates a vertical. Print infrastructure is exactly the kind of un-sexy, massive TAM he looks for.
**Focus:** Software eating vertical markets.

## 🇺🇸 United States (Global Infrastructure leaders)

### 11. Y Combinator (San Francisco)
**Why:** They fund a massive amount of "API for X" components. PrintPrice is essentially the "Stripe for Print Compliance/Preflight".
**Focus:** Everything, but huge on API automation.

### 12. Bessemer Venture Partners (Global)
**Why:** Authors of the annual "State of the Cloud" report. They defined vertical SaaS investing (e.g., Toast, Procore).
**Focus:** Cloud infrastructure, Vertical SaaS.

### 13. High Alpha (Indianapolis)
**Why:** The premier B2B SaaS venture studio and fund. If it's a B2B SaaS solving a massive workflow bottleneck, they are interested.
**Focus:** B2B SaaS exclusively.

### 14. Founders Fund (San Francisco)
**Why:** They love hard technical problems. A deterministic hybrid engine solving a massive physical logistics problem (printing) fits their "we wanted flying cars, we got 140 characters" thesis.
**Focus:** Deep tech, bold infrastructure.

### 15. FirstMark Capital (New York)
**Why:** Huge track record in eCommerce infrastructure (Shopify ecosystem, etc.). Print-on-demand is massive in eCommerce.
**Focus:** Infrastructure, eCommerce enablement.

### 16. Sequoia Capital (Global)
**Why:** Standard bearer. Arc (their seed vehicle) loves highly technical founders building "picks and shovels" for creators and platforms (Canva, Figma, etc.).
**Focus:** Market defining infrastructure.

### 17. Uncork Capital (San Francisco)
**Why:** Seed stage specialists in B2B SaaS, developer tools, and API-first businesses (SendGrid, Postmates).
**Focus:** B2B SaaS, DevTools.

### 18. Bowery Capital (New York)
**Why:** Exclusively focused on finding B2B software that updates legacy industries (like printing).
**Focus:** B2B SaaS driving legacy industry transformation.

### 19. Boldstart Ventures (New York)
**Why:** They define themselves as a "Day One" partner for enterprise infrastructure and SaaS developers.
**Focus:** Developer first, SaaS infra.

### 20. Costanoa Ventures (Palo Alto)
**Why:** They back early-stage founders transforming how business gets done, particularly in deeply specific industry verticals.
**Focus:** Enterprise, Vertical SaaS.

## 🤝 Strategic & Specialized (Print / Publishing / Design)

### 21. Gelato Ventures / Investment arm
**Why:** Gelato is one of the biggest POD networks globally. If PrintPrice solves preflighting for them, they are an instant acquirer/investor.
**Focus:** Strategic POD.

### 22. Vistaprint (Cimpress Ventures / Corporate Dev)
**Why:** The giant in the room. They acquire technology that improves their margins at scale.
**Focus:** Strategic Mass Customization.

### 23. Adobe Ventures
**Why:** Strategic interest. If you are doing what Acrobat Preflight does, but at headless AWS scale, you are on their radar.
**Focus:** Design, Document Infrastructure.

### 24. Shopify Ventures
**Why:** Massive amount of POD (Print on demand) happens on Shopify. Anything that reduces the friction for merchants launching POD brands is highly strategic.
**Focus:** Commerce Infrastructure.

### 25. Canva (Corporate Development)
**Why:** They recently acquired Affinity (design software) to go after professionals. A headless RIP-grade preflight engine is the missing link between Canva users and commercial print shops.
**Focus:** Democratizing design and publishing.
