## Why Now? The Automation Imperative

**1. The Explosion of Programmable Generation**
Ten years ago, PDFs were crafted by professional designers. Today, millions of PDFs are generated dynamically everyday by Canva, Figma, AI agents, and web-to-print portals. *The volume of print-ready assets is scaling exponentially, but the quality is dropping.*

**2. The Prepress Labor Crisis**
Skilled prepress operators are retiring, and print shops cannot hire fast enough to manually check the flood of generated files. Manual prepress has become the definitive unscalable bottleneck in an otherwise automated supply chain.

**3. Narrowing Margins in Print-on-Demand (POD)**
As POD and short-run commercial printing scale, margins rely entirely on throughput. A single manual touchpoint or a single ruined print run (due to unexpected RGB conversions or missing bleed) wipes out the margin for dozens of successful jobs.

**4. The Maturation of Cloud-Native Engines**
Legacy preflight tools (like PitStop) were built as desktop monoliths for humans to click through. Only now do we have the exact combination of containerized RIP engines (Ghostscript/Poppler) and scalable queue architectures (BullMQ/Redis) capable of creating a true, stateless API layer for prepress.
