# V26 Autonomous Global Print Infrastructure Constitution Spec v1.0.0

## 1. Introduction
The **Autonomous Global Print Infrastructure Constitution / Meta-Governance Layer (V26)** is the highest layer of PrintPrice OS. It defines the legitimacy of power, the boundaries of authority, and the safeguards that preserve institutional trust across the global network.

## 2. Constitutional Principles
The system operates under several foundational principles:
- **Bounded Authority**: No governance body has absolute or arbitrary power.
- **Due Process**: Every restriction or intervention requires evidence and a review path.
- **Evolution Legitimacy**: Changes to protocol or standards must follow versioned, governed paths.
- **Auditability of Power**: Governance actions are themselves subject to deterministic meta-audit.

## 3. Authority Domains
Powers are separated into distinct domains to prevent concentration:
- `operational_control`: Daily matchmaking and routing.
- `strategic_command`: High-level network optimization and intervention.
- `institutional_governance`: Standard maintenance and certification.
- `meta_governance`: Review of authority, legitimacy, and constitutional principles.

## 4. Emergency & Override Safeguards
Emergency powers are:
- **Trigger-Based**: Requires specific systemic risk (e.g., trust collapse).
- **Time-Bounded**: Automatically expires unless reviewed.
- **Reversible**: Designed to return to baseline operations through auditable rollback.

## 5. Participation Rights
Actors in the ecosystem (printers, publishers, brokers) have constitutional protections:
- Right to explanation.
- Right to evidence-backed decisions.
- Right to governed escalation and dispute resolution.
